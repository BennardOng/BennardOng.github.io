Growth stock - Simple Moving Average Analysis

Will holding a TLSA stock in its early growth stage help me attain my dream car?

Lets start by analysing the potential of implementing an algorithm system rather than an investing discretionary approach by weighting up the pros and cons. First, lets mention the stock spilts that occurred up to the date from June 29, 2010 to 27 September, 2022.

The stock spilts that occurred since TSLA went public

Telsa went public on June 29, 2010 at a price of $17 per share where there was a stock spilt on August 31, 2020 for 5 to 1 and another stock spilt occurred on August 25,2022 for a 3 for 1. This will equate to having a total of 15 stocks if the first purchase happened in June 29, 2010.

Total amount if a discretionary approach was used as an investment decision

If a discretionary approach was used to make an investment decision and was held until 27 September, 2022, the total amount of the investment from the 1 stock will be in total of USD $276.01 multiply by 15 stocks equaling USD $4140.15.
We make an assumption that the stock was sold at 27 September, 2022 and want to determine what will be the return on investment on the stock.
In percentage terms from a $17 investment and holding it until 27 September, 2022 will equal to a 243 times return which is a 24,300% increase.

Simple moving average algorithm system implemented on June 29, 2010

Lets assume I have the ability to time travel and knew TSLA would reach such heights that I would invest 1 share of TSLA and implement a simple moving average algorithm system and let the algorithm system decide on my entry based on technical indicators rather than a discretionary decision to understand the business overall objective from gathering information on the director, cash-flow, business objective and financial statements of the company.
